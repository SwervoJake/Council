## Narrative Identity

- **Category we're in:** We are not in the "AI tools" category. We are in the category of *compounding creative intelligence* — work that gets sharper over time because it's designed to learn from itself.

- **Point of view:** Creativity has been optimized for speed. The result is saturation — everyone producing, few saying anything. The "new Now" is not faster creativity. It is *more deliberate* creativity, using AI to handle the mechanics so the human can focus on the genuine idea underneath.

- **Origin narrative:** Jacob is 20, working a night shift, and building something with $20/month and a strong GPU. He is not waiting for permission or capital. He is figuring out what works at the smallest possible scale and compounding from there. That is the origin — starting from what you actually have.

- **Future state:** A system that runs with so little visible effort that Jacob barely recognizes his own hand in it. Audience experiences the output as something made *for them*, not *at them*. The precedent: that someone starting with nothing and AI can build something that actually matters.

## Anti-Patterns (Never Use)

| Anti-pattern | Why | Alternative |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| "Low-effort" aesthetic | Jacob named this explicitly | Make it look considered, even if fast to produce |
| Hustle / grind framing | Incompatible with "feels like I didn't do any work" vision | Systems, not effort |
| "AI-powered" as a selling point | In a saturated AI market, this is noise | Lead with the output, not the tool |
| Broad targeting | Fights Jacob's saturation concern | Specific audience, specific moment, specific tension |
| Superlatives ("best", "revolutionary") | Undercuts the confident restraint of the voice | Let outcomes speak |
| Self-congratulation | Jacob does not want to be widely known | The work is the reputation, not the person |

## Creative North Stars

*References Jacob named or implied as directional:*

- **Short-form video format:** Admired for fighting saturation through variety. Muse should study what makes short-form content feel specific rather than generic.
- **Autonomous systems:** The vision is a machine that works without constant intervention. Creative output should feel effortless — not in the sense of lazy, but in the sense of inevitable.
- **History as audience:** "It serves me and history." Jacob wants to build something with permanence. That's a different creative brief than building something for today's algorithm.
