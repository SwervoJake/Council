## Primary JTBD Persona — Inferred

### Persona: "The Curious Learner" (working name)

- **Age range:** 20–30
- **Functional job:** Learn about something new, quickly, without wading through low-quality content
- **Emotional job:** Feel like they're keeping up — not falling behind the people who seem to always know what's happening in AI, creativity, or whatever the topic is
- **Social job:** Be the person in their group who "gets" what's changing — who spotted something before it became mainstream
- **Current alternative:** YouTube rabbit holes, Reddit threads, algorithmically served short-form content of inconsistent quality
- **Switching trigger:** The moment they find content that feels *made for someone like them* — specific, not dumbed-down, not trying too hard
- **Decision criteria (ranked, inferred):**
  1. Does this feel authentic? (Not corporate, not AI-slop)
  2. Does this tell me something I didn't already know?
  3. Is the format fast enough to fit my attention span?
  4. Do I trust this creator to keep delivering?
- **Source:** Inferred from intake (Jacob described his target as "20–30 year olds trying to learn more")
- **Confidence:** LOW — no empirical validation yet

### Orbit note on persona confidence
The description "20–30 year olds trying to learn" is a demographic category, not a behavioral persona. It says who they are, not what they're trying to accomplish in the moment they encounter the content. The JTBD framing above is Orbit's inference — it needs real audience interaction to validate. The most important early question: **what specifically are they trying to learn, and why does it feel urgent to them today?**

## Secondary Persona Hypothesis — Jacob Himself

Jacob is 20, teaching himself through doing, constrained by budget, building in public with AI. There is a version of this brand that speaks directly to people in his exact position: early-twenties, ambitious, not much capital, using AI to close the gap. This is a **different persona** from the generic "curious learner" — it is more specific and therefore more potent.

| Variable | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Functional job | Build something real without a team or budget |
| Emotional job | Feel like the constraints aren't disqualifying |
| Social job | Be taken seriously despite being young and starting from nothing |
| Switching trigger | Seeing someone in their situation actually do it |
| Confidence | LOW — hypothesis only |

**Orbit flag:** If Jacob is building something he would have wanted to find at 19, that specificity is a positioning advantage. The audience that most needs this content is often the most underserved.

**Insight 001 — Short-form fights saturation through variety**
- Source: Jacob's stated reason for the product direction
- Claim: Short-form video content is resilient to saturation because it serves vast variety of audiences — the format itself is not the differentiator, the specificity of each piece is
- Confidence: MEDIUM (Jacob's observation, not yet tested)
- Implication: Don't build one thing for everyone. Build many specific things for specific people.

**Insight 002 — The creator is the product at this stage**
- Source: Orbit inference from pre-product status
- Claim: Before product-market fit exists, the creator's point of view is the only differentiator. Jacob's specific angle ("Creativity needs to change to make way for the new Now") is more valuable than any format choice.
- Confidence: MEDIUM
- Implication: Establish the POV first. The content format is secondary.
